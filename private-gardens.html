Rooted in Earth,Rooted in Faith.

In Islam, the garden is a place of reflection, gratitude, and connection with the natural world Allah has entrusted to us. Every home at Dar Al-Sakina includes its own private garden, designed for exactly this.

A Garden of Your Own

Your Own Private Sanctuary

Each home’s private garden is an enclosed, south-facing space opening from the main living area through wide sliding doors. It is entirely yours — to plant, to tend, to sit in silence, or to share with grandchildren visiting for Eid.


The gardens are low-maintenance by default, with raised beds for easy gardening without bending, a water feature for the soothing sound of flowing water, and a shaded pergola for the warmest Maryland afternoons.

Through the Year

A Garden for Every Season

🌸

Spring

Jasmine and rose bushes bloom alongside fruit trees. The garden awakens as Ramadan ends — a natural celebration of renewal.

☀️

Summer

Mint, thyme, and zaʼatar grow abundantly in raised planters. Mornings before Fajr are made fragrant by lavender along the garden path.

🍂

Autumn

Persimmon and fig trees bear fruit. Maryland’s golden autumn light filters through the pergola, making outdoor dhikr particularly beautiful.

❄️

Winter

Evergreen hedging keeps the garden private year-round. The heated pergola with glass panels lets you enjoy your garden even in the cold months.

Garden Features

What Every Garden Includes

01

Raised Planting Beds

Waist-height beds with accessible surround paths, so planting and harvesting never requires kneeling or uncomfortable bending.

02

Shaded Pergola & Seating

A timber pergola with climbing plants provides dappled shade for morning sitting, reading, or receiving guests.

03

Water Feature

A small recirculating fountain — a traditional element of Islamic garden design that invites calm throughout the day.

04

Fruit Tree of Your Choice

Each garden is planted with one fruit tree — fig, pomegranate, or apple — as a living reminder of divine provision.

05

Gate to Shared Courtyard

A private gate connects your garden directly to the community courtyard — community is always there, whenever you choose to step through.
