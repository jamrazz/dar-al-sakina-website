Our Mission

Built on the Foundations of Ummah

Dar Al-Sakina was born from a simple truth: our elders deserve more than comfort — they deserve belonging. We have created a place where neighbors become family, where the call to prayer echoes through shared courtyards, and where each day is shaped by the rhythms of faith, rest, and togetherness.


Located in Maryland, our community is designed for Muslim seniors who seek a peaceful, spiritually enriching environment surrounded by people who share their values and traditions.

The Heart of Our Community

The Shared Courtyard

At the center of Dar Al-Sakina lies a lush, landscaped courtyard — the living room of our community. Homes are arranged around it so every neighbor is close, every gathering is easy, and no one is ever truly alone.


The courtyard features shaded seating, a fountain, walking paths, fruit trees, and a central pavilion for Eid celebrations.

Shared Spaces

Everything a Fulfilling Life Needs

🕌

Community Prayer Hall

A dedicated musalla for the five daily prayers and Jumuʼah, with separate spaces for men and women and weekly Quran study circles.

🍽️

Halal Community Kitchen

A fully halal shared kitchen and dining area where residents cook together, share meals, and keep cultural food traditions alive.

🌺

Courtyard Garden

A landscaped central courtyard with a fountain, shaded seating, a community vegetable garden, and winding walking paths.

📚

Islamic Library & Reading Room

A quiet space stocked with books on Islamic scholarship, Arabic language, history, and leisure reading for reflective afternoons.

🩺

Wellness & Care Center

On-site wellness support including regular health screenings, visiting physicians, and care coordination for residents’ needs.

🎨

Arts & Activities Pavilion

A bright pavilion for calligraphy classes, Eid celebrations, community gatherings, and creative workshops year-round.

Daily Life

Days Shaped by Faith & Fellowship

Life at Dar Al-Sakina flows naturally with the rhythms of Islamic practice — grounded in prayer, enriched by community, and unhurried by design.

Fajr

Morning Prayer & Qurʼan

Community gathers in the musalla, followed by quiet recitation in the courtyard.

Morning

Garden Walks & Breakfast

A gentle walk, shared breakfasts, and morning conversation with neighbors.

Friday prayers and a communal midday meal bring everyone together.

Afternoon

Arabic lessons, calligraphy, storytelling circles, or peaceful rest in the courtyard.

Evening

Evening prayer followed by shared dinners — the warmest hour of every day.
