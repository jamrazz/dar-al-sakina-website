Our Mission

Built on the Foundations of Ummah

Dar Al-Sakina was born from a simple truth: our elders deserve more than comfort — they deserve belonging. We have created a place where neighbors become family, where the call to prayer echoes through shared courtyards, and where each day is shaped by the rhythms of faith, rest, and togetherness.


Located in Maryland, our community is designed for Muslim seniors who seek a peaceful, spiritually enriching environment surrounded by people who share their values and traditions.

The Design Philosophy

Rooted in Earth,Rooted in Faith.

In Islam, the garden is a place of reflection, gratitude, and connection with the natural world Allah has entrusted to us. Every home at Dar Al-Sakina includes its own private garden, designed for exactly this.

A Garden of Your Own

Your Own Private Sanctuary

Each home’s private garden is an enclosed, south-facing space opening from the main living area through wide sliding doors. It is entirely yours — to plant, to tend, to sit in silence, or to share with grandchildren visiting for Eid.


The gardens are low-maintenance by default, with raised beds for easy gardening without bending, a water feature for the soothing sound of flowing water, and a shaded pergola for the warmest Maryland afternoons.

Dar Al-Sakina› Community› Qibla-Oriented Design

Wherever You Stand,Mecca Is in Your Home.

Prayer is the pillar of the faith. At Dar Al-Sakina, every home is precisely oriented toward the qibla — with a dedicated prayer room, a built-in wudu station, and every detail designed for khushuʼ.

The Prayer Room

A Dedicated Space for Salah

In most homes, prayer happens in a corner of a bedroom — a space not designed for it. At Dar Al-Sakina, every home includes a dedicated prayer room. Not large, but purposeful. Designed for one thing: your connection with Allah.


The room is precisely oriented with the qibla wall marked by a built-in mihrab alcove. It includes a wudu station with an accessible basin, storage for prayer garments, and natural light that creates a calm, steady atmosphere at every hour of the day.

Design Philosophy

Three Principles of Islamic Space

🧭

Qibla Orientation

Each prayer room is calculated to the exact bearing for Mecca from our Maryland location — approximately 58° northeast. The mihrab wall is built at this precise angle. No compass needed.

💧

Wudu by Design

Purification before prayer is not an afterthought. Each room has an adjoining wudu station with a low-level accessible basin, non-slip floor, grab rail, and proper drainage — making tahara safe and dignified.

🌙

Light & Stillness

The prayer room receives diffused natural light through a high clerestory window, avoiding glare at Fajr or Dhuhr. Warm off-white walls aid concentration. Acoustic separation keeps the room quiet.

The Sacred Direction

The Kaaba — Heart of the Qibla

Every salah at Dar Al-Sakina faces the same point: the Kaaba in Mecca, the first house built for the worship of Allah.

Room by Room

A Walk Through the Prayer Space

01

Entry & Shoe Storage

A small vestibule at the entrance keeps outdoor shoes separate, maintaining the purity of the prayer space. A built-in bench with shoe storage below makes this transition easy for seniors.

02

The Wudu Station

An accessible low basin with lever-style taps, a grab rail, non-slip matting, and a fold-down seat. The wudu area is directly adjacent to the prayer room — you never walk far with wet feet.

03

The Mihrab Wall

A subtly recessed alcove in the qibla wall serves as the mihrab — a visual focal point and tactile marker of direction. Finished in textured plaster with a gentle arch, referencing Islamic tradition without excess.

04

A fitted alcove beside the mihrab stores rolled prayer rugs at accessible height, alongside a dedicated shelf for the Quran, tasbih, and prayer books — always at hand, never misplaced.

Independence WithoutCompromise.

Accessibility at Dar Al-Sakina is not a modification — it is the foundation. Every dimension, surface, and transition was designed from the start so that life can be lived fully, safely, and with complete dignity.

Universal Design

Designed for Life, Not Limitation

Our homes follow Universal Design principles — creating spaces usable by all people, at all ages and abilities, without adaptation. This means our accessibility features do not look like accessibility features. They look like good design.


A wide doorway is also an elegant doorway. A curbless shower is also a beautiful shower. Lever door handles are also easy for arthritic hands. Everything works for everyone — without compromise to beauty or dignity.

Our Commitment

Built Into Every Home

✓

Zero-Threshold Entry

Front door and all room transitions are flush — no lips, no ramps, no barriers anywhere.

✓

Wide-Passage Hallways

All corridors are a minimum 42 inches wide, allowing comfortable two-way movement with a walker or wheelchair.

✓

Reinforced Bathroom Walls

Walls are blocking-reinforced during construction so grab bars can be added anywhere, at any time, without opening walls.

✓

Smart Emergency System

Discreet pull-cord and button stations in the bathroom, bedroom, and front door, connected to our on-site wellness team.

✓

Non-Slip Flooring Throughout

All flooring is slip-rated for wet and dry conditions. Visual contrast between surfaces helps residents with low vision navigate confidently.

✓

Keyless Smart Entry

Keypad and app-based door entry eliminates keys for residents with limited dexterity, and lets trusted family members access remotely.

Common Questions

Payment FAQ

Is my payment information secure?

Yes. All card processing will be handled by Stripe, which is PCI DSS Level 1 compliant — the highest standard in payment security. We never store card numbers on our servers.

Are donations tax-deductible?

We are currently pursuing 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status. Donations are not currently tax-deductible, but we will notify all donors and provide amended receipts once our status is approved.

When is the monthly resident fee due?

Monthly resident fees are due on the 1st of each month. Auto-pay enrolled residents receive a reminder email 3 days prior. A 5-day grace period applies before any late fee is assessed.

Can I cancel a sadaqah subscription?

Yes — at any time, with no fees or penalties. Simply email [email protected] or cancel via the link in your monthly receipt email.

The form says “placeholder” — can I actually pay now?

The payment forms are design-ready placeholders pending integration with Stripe. To arrange payment immediately, please use the wire transfer details above or email our payments team directly.
